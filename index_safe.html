Maria Rugenstein


I'm a PhD Candidate in Reto Knutti's group at the Institute for Atmospheric and Climate Science at ETH Zürich and a visiting researcher in Ken Caldeira's group at the Carnegie Department of Global Ecology in Stanford. I'm interested in ocean heat storage and heat distribution, ocean dynamics, and how the ocean influences the atmosphere on large scales. I like thinking about first-order controls on Earth’s climate and the concept of climate forcings. I'm also interested in deep time paleo climate, internal variability, and the theory and sociology of science.

Right now my favorite project is LongRunMIP, a model intercomparison of millennia scale simulations of global coupled Atmosphere-Ocean Models.
